Transport Technology and Fabrication

Love working on boats, cars, buildings, or heavy machinery?
You could become an automotive engineer or technician working as a mechanic, repairing transmissions, brakes, steering and suspension.

Specialize in auto electronics and you’ll diagnose electrical faults and provide advice on electronic transmission and fuel injection. Taking to the water, boating is big in New Zealand – four in ten Kiwis own a boat. So there’s plenty of work designing, building and repairing them. Fabrication engineers and welders make and install metal parts like vents, handrails and boilers. They also make parts for aeroplanes and boats.

To become a boat builder, welder or car mechanic you usually need to complete an apprenticeship.
